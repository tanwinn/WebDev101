Forms

Alopex Lagopus (Arctic Fox)

As with many animals that inhabit the Arctic regions, the fur of the Arctic fox changes colour to adapt to its surroundings accordingly. In the winter, the Arctic fox has thick, white fur which allows the Arctic fox to remain warm and camouflaged in its snowy surroundings. In the summer months, the fur of the Arctic fox changes to a brown colour as there the snow will have melted. This newly coloured brown fur of the Arctic fox, allows the Arctic fox to remain as inconspicuous as possible whilst there is no snow in the Arctic during the summer months.

Poecilia (Molly)

Mollies are known for their calm and peaceful nature, which along with their brightly coloured bodies, makes them a particularly popular choice for freshwater tanks of all shapes and sizes. The male mollies are more slender than the female mollies and have a slightly longer tail fin, making the two sexes easy to tell apart.

Canis latrans (Coyote)

The coyote tends to reside in a burrow when it's not looking for food and the coyote is more than capable of digging the burrow itself. The coyote is intelligent enough to find and inhabit an empty badger burrow if at all possible. This becomes the centre of the coyote's territory which can extend to 19 km around the den. The size of the territory depends on how much food is available.

Saguinus Geoffroyi (Geoffroys Tamarin)

The Geoffroy's tamarin has a very distinctive "v" shaped mark on its head and has no fur on its face. The Geoffroy's tamarin is highly territorial and is known to make numerous different calls and threats to approaching danger in the surrounding forest.

Terms & Definition

Positioning in CSS

  • Static: default, following the document flow
  • Relative: to the normal/default page position; top 0px is the beginning of the page.
  • Absolute: targets to the position inside a relative element
  • Fixed: to the browser window, regardless if we scroll or resize the window, the position is fixed onto a specific posiiton
  • Initial: Set its properties to the default value
  • Inherit: inherit the parent's properties. Useful with margin, e.g. cause margin inherits from root by default and not the parent.
